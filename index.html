About the Festival

Sustainable development always encourages us to conserve and enhance our resources, by gradually changing the manners in which we develop and use technologies. All Countries should meet their basic needs of employment, food, energy, water, and sani- tation.With the right tools and information, children and young people can play a critical role in the implementation and monitoring of the SDGs, spark- ing action in their communities. Educating and engaging young people on why the goals matter to their everyday lives is the need of the hour.

We do this through our flagship initiative: GYFF i.e. the Global Youth Film Festival.

It gives us immense pleasure to introduce you to the Global Youth Film Festival- 2023. What does a story about global progress look like? That's the question that we are addressing through the creativity and skills of our nation's young minds. Storytelling can go a long way in capturing people's imagination, and what's more, inspire the kind of action that lasts. Showcasing stories through Films that illustrate youth and children working to build a better future and realize the importance of the Sustainable Development Goals is at the heart of the GYFF 2023.

The said event will commence on National Youth Day i.e. 12th JANUARY and will conclude on 14th JANUARY, 2023 at the Leisure Valley, Sector-10, Chandigarh, Aspiring Youngsters across the country are invited to this MegaFest. The Students will be given a chance to present their short film to a repu- table Jury on stage: The audience will consist of VIPS from Administration, Universities, Colleges, Schools & general audience.

What are Sustainable Development Goals?

Sustainable development is an organizing principle that aims to meet human development goals while also enabling natural systems to provide necessary natural resources and ecosystem services to humans. The desired result is a society where living conditions and resources meet human needs without undermining the planetary integrity and stability of the natural system. The Brundtland Report in 1987 defined sustainable development as "development that meets the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs". The concept of sustainable development nowadays has a focus on economic development, social development and environmental protection for future generations.
